Who we are

Stokisha is a first of its kind, a digital lending platform in the region whose key objective is to provide an enabling economic environment for ethical wealth creation among the youth, women and SMEs through adoption of electronic credit facilities for their businesses.

Our Mission

Unlock the potential of small business owners, particularly to enable women, youth & persons living with disabilities, to access affordable and short-term ethical SME financing. This will in turn drive positive socio-economic change in their communities, families & the region.

Our Objectives

Our product has been tailored with the following objectives in mind; Ethical Financing tailored for SMEs Empowering women, youth & persons living with disabilities. Capacity building of SMEs. Driving positive social impact.

Core Values

Enabling Accessibility Simplicity. Fairness

Our Vision

To be a differentiated and unique digital mobile loans provider focused on small business growth & poverty alleviation.

Sectors

Agribusiness

Register for an account. If you already have an account with us simply login.

Agribusiness is the backbone of the Kenyan economy, the sector alone employs 62% of the country’s population.

Despite this, there is still a persistent absence of accurate and cost-effective methods for assessing small-scale agricultural lending risk associated with small holder farmers responsible for 70% of Kenya’s Agricultural production. The smaller agribusiness SMEs are unable to access financing due to four factors;

  • Lack of credible and verifiable data to support Lending
  • Lack of Collateral.
  • Risk Consideration due to inherent agricultural risk.

    • At Stokisha it is our aim to challenge this status quo.


Trade

The Wholesale and Retail Sector rounds up the top 4 contributors to Kenya’s GDP.

According to a recent report on this segment, Kenya’s retail spending hit an all time high of Kes 1.8 trillion. A few factors hold true with regards to the wholesale and retail segment and SMEs.

  • Kenya is a credit market and over 80% of retail trade is credit oriented.
  • The bigger opportunity is in General and Informal retail sector - shops & kiosks.
  • Know your customer (KYC) is an impediment although licensing requirements have seen significant rise in registration.
  • Last mile credit facilities availed by the general trade in some instances.

Manufacturing

Manufacturing is a key driver of the government’s Big four agenda and receives only 10% of financing from Kenya’s banks.

Despite the potential identified in this sector, there is still a huge gap in terms of financing. This can be attributed to:

  • Mismatch between financing needs and what banks are willing to offer. - Working capital cycle for manufacturing is longer than 6 months.
  • Perceived risk arising from a few bad lending decisions.
  • High interest rates.

Services

Target Marketgrowth

Stokisha, as a product has been designed to drive financial empowerment specifically towards women entrepreneurs, youth (individuals aged 18 – 35) and persons living with disabilities who run small businesses.

The Youth

The youth are an integral part of our mission and vision. This is largely attributed to the pivotal role they play in future economic development. The proportion of Kenya’s youth to the population is among the highest globally, presenting the economy with a vibrant manpower if put to productive use.

Women in entrepreneurship

Most informal businesses especially in the SME sector are run by women. It is through these same businesses that they manage the family unit; most African societies are matriarchal in relation to day-day running of the household.

Persons with disabilities

At Stokisha we believe that disability is not inability as we seek to provide financial inclusion and equal opportunity for all regardless of any disability and we encourage persons living with disabilities to be part of the economic growth by funding their small businesses.
